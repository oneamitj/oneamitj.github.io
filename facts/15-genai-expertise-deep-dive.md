## GenAI Philosophy & Views
From his "Dawn of the Agents" presentation and ArchMentor platform:
- Foundation models are infrastructure — they're becoming commoditized
- Don't build custom models unless you have a compelling, measured business case
- "For most teams, custom models are a vendor-lock-in shaped hammer looking for a nail"
- Design for model plurality — don't lock into a single provider
- Agents (with memory, tools, policies, autonomy) are the real application layer
- "Agents ≠ Chatbots. Agents = long-lived AI workers"
- Treat agents like junior engineers: narrow tasks, guardrails, supervised outcomes
- Over-invest in validation — tests are more important than ever
- "If you can't explain why an agent did something, you can't run it in production"
- Security starts at prompt time, not post-deployment
- "Productivity is up. Visibility is down." — shipping faster but accumulating complexity
