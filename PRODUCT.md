# Product

## Register

brand

## Platform

web

## Users

Hiring managers, CTOs, and recruiters evaluating a senior Solution Architect; prospective clients and collaborators in GenAI/cloud consulting; fellow engineers arriving from articles, talks, or GitHub. They land with a "who is this person?" question and 30 seconds of attention, usually from a LinkedIn or search referral, often on mobile.

## Product Purpose

Personal portfolio for Amit Joshi (oneamitj) — Solution Architect specializing in the intersection of high-scale AWS cloud infrastructure and GenAI, based in Melbourne. The site exists to convert a casual visit into a conversation: hire, consult, collaborate, or talk. Success = the visitor feels "I want to reach out to this person" and acts on it (LinkedIn first, CV/email second).

## Positioning

The architect who takes GenAI from demo to regulated, production-grade reality — 10+ years of AWS infrastructure depth fused with 10+ shipped LLM systems, HIPAA-compliant and cost-optimized.

## Conversion & proof

- Primary CTA: Reach out on LinkedIn (linkedin.com/in/oneamitj). Secondary: view /cv and download the PDF resume.
- The line a visitor remembers after 10 seconds: "This person builds production GenAI on AWS — and I should talk to him."
- Belief ladder: (1) this is a serious, current, frontier-level engineer → (2) he has real production depth, not demos: metrics, compliance, scale → (3) respected people vouch for him emphatically → (4) he's warm and easy to work with → reach out.
- Proof on hand: three glowing LinkedIn recommendations (VP/Head of AI, ex-McKinsey VP, AWS VP) in linkedin-info/Recommendations.md; AWS GenAI + DevOps Competency and APN Advanced membership (team lead); CIO.com webcast speaker with AWS; hard metrics (10x throughput, 50% cost cuts, 150K+ concurrent users, 10+ production LLM systems); published articles; 10 years of named employers and projects.

## Brand Personality

Visionary / AI-native, calm enterprise authority, hands-on builder, warm & approachable — all four, balanced. Voice: confident and specific, never boastful; numbers over adjectives; a visible undercurrent of curiosity and play (the terminal easter egg, watchmaking, side projects). Emotional goal on landing: awe first, trust immediately after.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS-template portfolios: hero-metric grids, identical card walls, stock "developer at laptop" imagery.
- AI-slop landing pages: gradient text, glassmorphism everywhere, eyebrow labels above every section.
- The old site's failure mode in reverse: pure novelty (terminal-only) that hides the substance from non-technical visitors. The new site leads with substance, keeps the play discoverable.

## Design Principles

1. Awe, then evidence — a cinematic first impression must hand off to concrete proof (metrics, logos, recommendations) within one scroll.
2. Show the craft — the site itself is the first exhibit of "this person ships polished, modern systems"; motion and detail quality are part of the argument.
3. Numbers over adjectives — every claim carries a metric, a name, or an artifact.
4. Play is discoverable, not default — the terminal, easter eggs, and humor reward curiosity without gating content.
5. Every path ends at a person — sections resolve toward contact; reaching out should never be more than one glance away.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA contrast on the dark theme; full keyboard navigability; `prefers-reduced-motion` alternatives for every animation (crossfade or instant); semantic HTML retained for the SEO/no-JS fallback; readable without JavaScript.
